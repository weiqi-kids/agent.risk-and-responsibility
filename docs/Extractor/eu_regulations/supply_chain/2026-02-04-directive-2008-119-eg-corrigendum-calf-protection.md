## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member States, agricultural operators, calf farmers, veterinary authorities, animal welfare inspectors
- **shift_type**: clarified
- **shift_summary**: Clarifies the age threshold for rough fodder requirements in calf nutrition, changing from "from the second week of life" to "for each calf over two weeks old"

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Supply chain responsibility (animal welfare standards)
- Agricultural compliance
- Veterinary and health standards

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Calves' daily feed rations must contain sufficient iron to maintain average hemoglobin levels of at least 4.5 mmol/l blood
  - Specified minimum rough fodder quantities must be provided based on calf age (over two weeks old)
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Documentation of feed composition and iron content
  - Blood hemoglobin level measurements
  - Age-specific feeding records for calves
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
The corrigendum does not concern the English version - the correction applies to German language text. This modification clarifies existing obligations rather than creating new ones. As this is a corrigendum to a Directive, it requires transposition into national law by Member States.
