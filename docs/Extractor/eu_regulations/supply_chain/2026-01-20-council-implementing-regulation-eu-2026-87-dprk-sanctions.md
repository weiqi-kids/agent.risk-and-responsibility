## L1 — Rule Signal

- **document_type**: Regulation
- **binding_force**: directly_applicable

- **rule_type**: Implementing Regulation (EU)
- **entry_into_force**: 2026-01-13 (day following publication on 2026-01-12)
- **scope**: EU-wide restrictive measures (sanctions list update) concerning Guatemala
- **target_jurisdiction**: All EU Member States (directly applicable)
- **change_nature**: Amendment to existing sanctions framework - updates Annex I entries for five individuals under Regulation (EU) 2024/287

## L2 — Responsibility Structure

- **affected_roles**:
  - Financial institutions conducting customer due diligence and sanctions screening
  - Compliance officers managing sanctions lists
  - Payment service providers processing transactions
  - Export control and trade compliance teams
  - Legal and risk functions overseeing restrictive measures compliance
- **shift_type**: clarified
- **shift_summary**: Organizations must update their sanctions screening systems to reflect revised entries for five Guatemalan officials, including updated position titles and reasons for designation related to undermining democracy and rule of law in Guatemala

## L3 — Risk Domains

- **primary_risk**: Sanctions breach (failure to screen against updated designations)
- **secondary_risks**:
  - Reputational damage from transactions with designated persons
  - Financial penalties for non-compliance with EU restrictive measures
  - Asset freeze violations if existing relationships not identified
- **cross_border_dimension**: Yes - EU sanctions apply extraterritorially to EU persons and entities regardless of location; impacts Guatemala-related business activities
- **supply_chain_relevance**: Medium - affects financial transactions and business relationships with designated Guatemalan officials; may impact supply chain partners with connections to listed individuals

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence

- **new_obligations**:
  - Update sanctions screening databases to include revised Annex I entries for five individuals by entry into force date (2026-01-13)
  - Screen existing customers and business partners against updated list
  - Freeze assets of designated persons if identified
  - Prohibit making funds or economic resources available to listed individuals
  - Report any frozen assets or transactions to competent national authorities
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Timestamped sanctions list updates in compliance systems
  - Screening logs demonstrating retroactive checks against updated entries
  - Asset freeze documentation if designated persons identified
  - Training records showing sanctions compliance team awareness of update
  - Audit trail of list version control and implementation
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes

- **Base regulation**: This implements Regulation (EU) 2024/287 of 12 January 2024 concerning restrictive measures in Guatemala
- **Nature of update**: Technical amendment updating biographical information and justifications for five existing designations (not adding new persons)
- **Affected sectors**: The five individuals are associated with: public prosecution office, judicial system, and civil society organizations
- **Context**: Sanctions relate to actions undermining democracy, rule of law, and judicial independence in Guatemala
- **Cross-reference**: Organizations should verify their screening systems incorporate not just names but also position updates, as designation reasons reference official roles
- **Filename discrepancy**: The filename references "DPRK sanctions" but this regulation concerns Guatemala - filename should be corrected for accurate cataloging
