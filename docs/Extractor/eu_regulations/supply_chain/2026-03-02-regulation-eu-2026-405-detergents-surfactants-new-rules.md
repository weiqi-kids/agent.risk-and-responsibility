## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Manufacturers, importers, distributors, authorised representatives, market surveillance authorities, customs authorities, end-users
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Replaces the 2004 detergents regulation with modernised, harmonised rules covering biodegradability, phosphorus limits, digital product passports, and microorganism safety, applying from 22 March 2026.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Supply chain (chemical product compliance)
- Environmental and health protection
- Digital market (digital product passport requirement)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Surfactants must comply with biodegradability standards; films by 2032 and other organic substances by 2034
  - Phosphorus content limits apply to consumer laundry and dishwasher detergents
  - Products containing micro-organisms must meet specific safety requirements
  - Animal testing for compliance is prohibited except in exceptional circumstances
  - Digital product passports must be created for detergents and end-user surfactants
  - Technical documentation must be maintained for 10 years
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Technical documentation demonstrating compliance with biodegradability and safety standards
  - Digital product passport containing required product information
  - Records supporting phosphorus content compliance
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
