## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member States, farmers (small and organic), agricultural authorities, payment agencies
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Extends permanent grassland reclassification periods, exempts small farmers from conditionality requirements, and introduces crisis payment mechanisms for agricultural disasters

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Supply chain resilience (agricultural crisis payments)
- Environmental compliance (organic farming recognition)
- Administrative burden (simplified procedures)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Member States may extend grassland reclassification period from 5 to 7 years
  - Small farmers receiving payments under Article 28 are exempted from GAEC standards and SMRs
  - Certified organic farmers presumed compliant with GAEC standards 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7
  - Crisis payments enabled following natural disasters, adverse climate events, or catastrophic occurrences
  - Strategic amendments require Commission approval; non-strategic amendments proceed by notification only
  - Maximum payment thresholds increased with €75,000 lump-sum options for small farm interventions
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Grassland use documentation for 5-7 year period
  - Small farmer certification for conditionality exemption
  - Organic farming certification for GAEC presumption
  - Crisis event documentation for payment eligibility
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Annual performance clearance under Article 54 of Regulation 2021/2116 is deleted. Provisions regarding annual performance reports and performance clearance apply to agricultural financial year 2025 and subsequent years. Member States may use calculation methods from Regulation (EU) 2021/1060 without additional justification.
