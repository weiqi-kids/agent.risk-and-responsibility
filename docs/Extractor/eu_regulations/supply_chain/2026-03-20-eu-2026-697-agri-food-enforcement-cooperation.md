## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member State enforcement authorities designated under Directive 2019/633, buyers and suppliers in agricultural and food supply chains, European Commission
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes a new cross-border cooperation framework requiring Member State enforcement authorities to exchange information and coordinate investigations into unfair trading practices spanning the agricultural and food supply chain.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Supply chain enforcement and cross-border coordination
- Unfair trading practices in agricultural and food sectors
- Regulatory compliance for buyers and suppliers

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Enforcement authorities must cooperate to prevent or stop unfair trading practices with a cross-border dimension
  - Authorities must exchange information within 90 days of requests
  - Designated coordinators must oversee investigations into widespread practices spanning three or more Member States
  - Member States must ensure enforcement authorities have adequate resources and expertise
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Reasonable suspicion of widespread practices required before coordinated actions are launched
  - Information sharing subject to confidentiality protections per Article 6(3)
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
