## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU High Representative, Conflict Armament Research (CAR), EU Member States, national authorities in conflict-affected countries, UN bodies, INTERPOL, Europol
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Continues and expands the iTrace programme (sixth phase) to monitor and report on illicit conventional arms diversion and trafficking across 40+ conflict-affected states, with EUR 6,000,000 EU contribution (total EUR 8,400,000 with co-financing).

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Illicit arms trafficking and diversion
- Supply chain security for conventional weapons and ammunition
- Export control compliance
- Conflict zone security monitoring

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Conflict Armament Research (CAR) must maintain an accessible information system on diverted weapons
  - CAR must provide training to national authorities in conflict-affected states
  - 24-hour help desk service for EU Member State consultations required
  - Production of policy reports on trafficking patterns required
  - Quarterly reporting to EU required
  - Politically balanced reporting and transparent trace processes with 28-day government response periods mandated
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Quarterly operational reports submitted to High Representative
  - Co-financing documentation (EU EUR 6M of total EUR 8.4M)
  - Operations must cover 40+ conflict-affected states
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
