## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Manufacturers and importers of detergents/surfactants, distributors, refill retailers, market surveillance authorities, customs authorities, online marketplaces
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Replaces 2004 legislation (Regulation EC No 648/2004) with comprehensive new obligations including digital product passports, enhanced biodegradability and phosphorus limits, safety requirements for micro-organisms, and explicit refill and online sales compliance rules.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Product safety and chemical substance regulation
- Supply chain transparency and traceability
- Environmental compliance (biodegradability, phosphorus limits)
- Digital infrastructure for market surveillance

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Manufacturers must create and maintain digital product passports accessible via data carriers before market placement
  - Biodegradability requirements for surfactants and polymeric films in detergent capsules must be met
  - Phosphorus limits apply to consumer laundry and dishwasher detergents
  - Safety assessment and risk assessment required before marketing products containing intentionally added micro-organisms
  - Ingredients data sheets must be submitted to appointed bodies for emergency health response before market release
  - Enhanced labelling requirements including fragrance allergens and preservatives
  - Refill stations must comply with safety standards
  - Online marketplace operators must ensure distance sales compliance
  - Animal testing prohibition: no new formulations may use animal test data
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Digital product passport records accessible to competent authorities and market surveillance bodies
  - Biodegradability test documentation per updated methodologies
  - Safety assessment reports for micro-organism-containing products
  - Ingredient data sheets filed with appointed emergency response bodies
  - Labelling compliance documentation
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
WebFetch successful. This is a significant product regulation overhaul repealing Regulation (EC) No 648/2004. Most provisions apply from 2027-03-23 (one year after entry into force), giving manufacturers a transitional period. Biodegradability criteria for polymers (Article 32) and organic substances (Article 34) have extended timelines of 2032–2034. The digital product passport requirement aligns with the EU's broader Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation framework. Manufacturers with global supply chains supplying EU markets should begin compliance planning immediately.
