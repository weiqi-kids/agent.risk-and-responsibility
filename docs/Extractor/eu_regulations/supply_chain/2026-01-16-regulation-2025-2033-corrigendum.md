## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Union telecommunications operators, nuclear energy operators, entities involved in non-military nuclear cooperation, medical radioisotope producers
- **shift_type**: clarified
- **shift_summary**: Corrigendum corrects Article 5id(10)(f) to clarify permitted activities under sanctions regime - changes scope from telecommunications services to non-military nuclear cooperation activities including installation, operation, maintenance of nuclear facilities, medical radioisotope production, and environmental radiation monitoring

## L3 — Risk Domains
- **Sanctions Compliance**: Significant correction to permitted activities under Russia sanctions framework affects compliance obligations
- **Nuclear Energy Cooperation**: Explicitly permits non-military nuclear activities including facility construction, fuel supply, and research
- **Medical Services**: Permits precursor materials for medical radioisotope production and medical applications
- **Environmental Monitoring**: Allows critical technology for environmental radiation monitoring

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**: Entities must comply with corrected scope of permitted activities - telecommunications services exception replaced by nuclear energy cooperation exception
- **evidence_requirements**: Documentation demonstrating non-military nature of nuclear cooperation activities
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This is the third corrigendum (R(03)) to Council Regulation (EU) 2025/2033 amending Regulation (EU) No 833/2014 on restrictive measures against Russia. The correction fundamentally reframes an exception provision from telecommunications services to nuclear energy cooperation, representing a substantive policy adjustment to the sanctions framework. Published in Official Journal L 2025/91053 on 30 December 2025, available in all 24 EU official languages.
