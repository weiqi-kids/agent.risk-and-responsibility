## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Spain (3 allocated tuna purse seiner vessels), France (1 allocated tuna purse seiner vessel), EU Member States fisheries management authorities, EU Commission (Protocol negotiator), Cook Islands Government fisheries management
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes vessel allocation framework under the 2025-2032 Sustainable Fisheries Partnership Agreement Protocol with Cook Islands, allocating 4 tuna purse seiners total (3 to Spain, 1 to France) with provisional application from 9 December 2025

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Fishing quota compliance and allocation management between Member States
- Supply chain continuity risk (minimizing interruption of fishing activities during Protocol transition)
- International agreement execution and provisional application legal risk
- Marine resource sustainability and exclusive fishing zone access rights

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**: Member States must respect allocated vessel quotas (Spain: 3 tuna purse seiners, France: 1 tuna purse seiner); Member States must manage fishing opportunities within Cook Islands exclusive fishing zone according to Protocol specifications; Member States must ensure compliance with provisional application terms from 9 December 2025
- **evidence_requirements**: Vessel registration and allocation records demonstrating compliance with quota distribution; fishing activity logs within Cook Islands waters; documentation of Protocol compliance under provisional application framework
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
- Protocol operates under provisional application (Article 13) from 9 December 2025 pending formal conclusion, enabling continuity of EU fishing vessel activities
- Regulation enters into force the day following publication (24 December 2025) but applies retroactively from 9 December 2025
- Previous Protocol expired 16 December 2024; this regulation minimizes interruption period
- Binding in entirety and directly applicable to all Member States without transposition
- Council authorized negotiations September 2024, Protocol initialled 20 June 2025, signed 9 December 2025
- No explicit enforcement mechanisms or penalties detailed within regulation text; enforcement falls under broader EU fisheries regulations
