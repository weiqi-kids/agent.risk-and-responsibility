## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Union compulsory licenses cover patents, utility models, and supplementary protection certificates
  - Licenses granted only when declared crisis/emergency mode exists under specified Union mechanisms
  - Right holder must have opportunity to submit comments before license grant
  - Licenses must be non-exclusive, non-assignable, and strictly limited to crisis scope and duration
  - Adequate remuneration required considering expected revenue, R&D costs, public support, and humanitarian factors
  - Strict export prohibitions for products manufactured under Union compulsory licenses
  - Customs authorities conduct risk-based monitoring with 10-day compliance determination
  - Competent advisory body assists Commission by identifying IP rights, disseminating notices, and assessing licensee capacity
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Evidence that protected inventions are necessary for adequate product supply
  - Finding that voluntary agreements could not succeed within reasonable timeframes
  - Demonstration of licensee capacity for swift exploitation
  - Compliance documentation for export controls
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This regulation functions as a last resort when voluntary agreements cannot ensure adequate supply of crisis-relevant products. The framework excludes defense-related products and does not supersede copyright, trade secret, or related rights protections. Union compulsory licenses automatically expire when the crisis or emergency mode ends. Geographic limitation strictly confined to the Union.
