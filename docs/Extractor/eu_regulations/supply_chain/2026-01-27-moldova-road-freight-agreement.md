- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: The Decision extends the existing EU-Ukraine road freight framework until mid-2025 with automatic renewal option, maintaining temporary EU-level regulation of cross-border road transport that would otherwise fall under Member State competence

## L3 — Risk Domains

- **supply_chain**: Cross-border freight continuity between EU and Ukraine during conflict period; risk of service disruption if agreement lapses without renewal
- **regulatory_compliance**: Operators must comply with extended bilateral framework; uncertainty around post-June 2025 renewal decisions
- **operational**: Road haulage operators face planning challenges due to six-month renewal cycles rather than multi-year certainty
- **geopolitical**: Agreement sustainability depends on continuation of "Solidarity Lanes" policy and evolving EU-Ukraine relations

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence

- **new_obligations**:
  - EU Commission must notify Ukraine of consent to be bound by amending Agreement
  - Road haulage operators continue operations under extended bilateral framework until at least 2025-06-30
  - Member States accept temporary cessation of individual competence in this transport sector
  - Parties must monitor tacit renewal deadlines to prevent unintended agreement expiry

- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Commission notification to Ukraine (diplomatic communication record)
  - Compliance with bilateral agreement terms as documented in original 2022-06-29 Agreement
  - Monitoring of renewal decision timelines and notifications

- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
  - Union competence ceases when Agreement expires, reverting authority to Member States
  - No explicit penalties mentioned; enforcement relies on diplomatic-level agreement adherence
  - Provisional application since 2024-06-20 suggests operational enforcement already active

## Notes

- **Document Note**: This Decision references an amending Agreement to the original EU-Ukraine road freight Agreement of 2022-06-29. The substantive obligations are in the underlying Agreement, not the Decision itself.
- **CELEX Reference Anomaly**: The document title in EUR-Lex references "Moldova" but content clearly addresses Ukraine. This may be a database indexing issue or the URL provided may have redirected.
- **Temporal Structure**: The Decision entered force in July 2025 but governs an Agreement provisionally applied since June 2024 and valid until June 2025 — creating overlapping temporal layers.
- **Competence Signal**: Explicit temporary exercise of Union competence with automatic reversion clause is notable for federal-level governance watchers.
