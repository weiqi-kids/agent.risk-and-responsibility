## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member states, commercial fishing vessels, recreational fisheries, regional fisheries management organizations
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes catch limits and fishing effort restrictions for 2026-2028, including mandatory six-month commercial eel closures and specific seabass quotas for sustainable yield objectives

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Supply chain management
- Environmental compliance
- Resource sustainability

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Manage fishing quotas according to scientific advice prioritizing sustainable yield
  - Implement at least six months annual closure for commercial eel fishing
  - Spain and France jointly ensure seabass removals do not exceed 3,883 tonnes in designated areas
  - Comply with recreational fishing limits (e.g., maximum three seabass specimens per angler daily in certain zones)
  - Apply restrictions across Atlantic, Mediterranean, and Pacific fishing zones managed by regional organizations
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Catch reporting and quota compliance documentation
  - Closure period enforcement records
  - Regional fisheries management organization cooperation records
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Retroactive application from 1 December 2025 for certain provisions. Applies to Union fishing vessels, third-country vessels in Union waters, and specified recreational fisheries.
