## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU Member States, commercial fishing operators, recreational fishers, national fisheries authorities, European Commission
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes total allowable catches (TACs) and quota allocations for 2026-2028 across multiple fish stocks, with landing obligations and conservation measures

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Sustainable resource management
- Environmental compliance
- Supply chain transparency (catch reporting)
- International fisheries cooperation
- Biodiversity conservation

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Member States must comply with allocated quotas for each fish stock
  - Commercial fishing operators subject to TAC limits, gear restrictions, and seasonal closures
  - Recreational fishers limited by catch quantity restrictions for specified species (seabass, pollack, eel)
  - National fisheries authorities must enforce and monitor compliance
  - Implement landing obligations for regulated stocks
  - Manage high-risk stocks through by-catch TACs and conservation measures
  - Report catch data to Commission
  - Apply maximum sustainable yield (MSY) principles
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Catch reporting documentation
  - Quota utilization records
  - Compliance monitoring reports
  - Landing documentation
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This regulation amends Regulation (EU) 2025/202 and applies retroactively from 1 January 2026. It implements scientific advice from ICES and coordinates with multiple international fisheries organizations (NEAFC, ICCAT, NAFO). Category assigned as supply_chain due to focus on sustainable resource extraction, quota management, and traceability requirements.
