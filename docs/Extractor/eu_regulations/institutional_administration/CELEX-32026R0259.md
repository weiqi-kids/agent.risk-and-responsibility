## What Changed

This implementing regulation amends Annex I to Regulation (EU) 2024/2642 by adding six natural persons to the EU sanctions list targeting individuals engaged in Russian destabilization activities through information manipulation and propaganda.

**Article 1** mandates Annex I amendment in accordance with attached list.

**Article 2** establishes immediate entry into force upon Official Journal publication and confirms binding application across all Member States.

### Newly Listed Natural Persons (6)

All six individuals designated for "implementing, supporting and benefiting from actions and policies attributable to the Government of the Russian Federation which undermine or threaten democracy, the rule of law, stability and security in the Union and a third country (Ukraine), through the use of information manipulation and interference."

1. **Dmitry Viktorovich Guberniev** - TV presenter/journalist spreading propaganda supporting Ukraine invasion
2. **Ekaterina Sergeevna Andreeva** - News anchor disseminating disinformation about the war
3. **Maria Eduardovna Sittel** - News presenter hosting propaganda events
4. **Pavel Aleksandrovich Zarubin** - Journalist with exclusive Putin access promoting anti-Ukrainian messaging
5. **Roman Vasilyevich Chumakov** - Actor/singer supporting Russian armed forces
6. **Sergey Vladimirovich Polunin** - Ballet dancer promoting "Russian world" doctrine

## Who Must Act

**EU Member States**: Implement asset freezes and restrictive measures against listed persons immediately.

**Financial institutions**: Screen transactions and freeze assets of designated individuals engaged in Russian propaganda operations.

**Media monitoring authorities**: Track activities of listed individuals in information manipulation campaigns.

**Border authorities**: Enforce travel restrictions on designated persons.

## Shift in Risk/Responsibility

**Shift Type**: expanded

**What responsibility is transferred/created**:
- Financial institutions must expand sanctions screening to include six new propaganda and disinformation operatives
- Media platforms and broadcasters face increased scrutiny for content featuring listed individuals
- Cultural institutions must conduct due diligence on partnerships involving designated artists/performers
- Compliance officers must monitor information manipulation activities beyond traditional military/defense sectors

**Enforcement Signal**: mandatory

**Justification cited**:
Listed individuals are responsible for:
- Spreading propaganda supporting Russia's invasion of Ukraine
- Disseminating disinformation about the war
- Hosting state-sponsored propaganda events
- Promoting narratives undermining democracy and rule of law
- Supporting Russian armed forces through cultural/media influence
- Advancing "Russian world" ideology justifying territorial aggression

The regulation explicitly targets information manipulation and interference as destabilizing activities threatening "democracy, the rule of law, stability and security in the Union and a third country (Ukraine)."
