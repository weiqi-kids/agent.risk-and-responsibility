# Council Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/267 - Iran Human Rights Sanctions

## 基本資訊

**CELEX 編號**: 32026R0267

**官方公報**: OJ L, 2026/267, 29.1.2026

**通過日期**: 2026-01-29

**生效日期**: Upon publication

**法規類型**: **regulation**

**約束力等級**: **directly_applicable**

**法律基礎**: Article 12(1) of Council Regulation (EU) No 359/2011 concerning restrictive measures for Iran human rights situation

## 規範主題

Amends Annex I to Regulation (EU) No 359/2011 by adding 15 individuals and 6 entities to EU sanctions list for serious human rights violations including protest suppression, arbitrary detention, torture oversight, and internet censorship.

## 責任轉移態樣

**轉移類型**: **expanded**

**執行信號**: **mandatory**

**受規範對象**:
- 15 Iranian officials (ministers, prosecutors, judges, IRGC commanders, security force leaders)
- 6 Iranian censorship and surveillance entities
- EU Member States (enforcement authorities)
- EU economic operators and financial institutions

**新增責任內容**:

### 1. Designated Individuals (15 Persons)

**High-Level Officials**:
- **Eskandar Momeni**: Minister of Interior - role in suppressing protests
- **Mohammad Movahedi-Azad**: Prosecutor General - arbitrary detention oversight

**IRGC and Security Forces**:
- IRGC commanders involved in protest suppression
- Security force leaders responsible for arbitrary arrests and detention

**Judiciary**:
- Judges overseeing torture and arbitrary detention cases
- Prosecutors involved in politically motivated prosecutions

**All individuals listed for**: Roles in suppressing protests, arbitrary detention, torture oversight, and/or coordinating repression campaigns

### 2. Designated Entities (6 Organizations)

**Censorship and Regulatory Bodies**:
- **Iranian Audio-Visual Media Regulatory Authority (SATRA)**: State media censorship
- **Working Group for Determining Instances of Criminal Content (WGDICC)**: Content criminalization body

**Surveillance and Cyber Operations**:
- **Seraj Cyberspace Organization**: Cyber surveillance
- **Yaftar Pazhohan Pishtaz Rayanesh Limited Company**: Surveillance technology provider
- **Douran Software Technologies**: Software for censorship/surveillance
- **Masaf Institute**: Research supporting disinformation and suppression

**All entities designated for**: Censorship, surveillance, disinformation, and suppression activities

### 3. Enforcement Obligations

**Asset Freezes**:
- All funds and economic resources of listed persons/entities frozen
- Prohibition on making funds/resources available directly or indirectly

**Transaction Prohibitions**:
- EU operators cannot engage in transactions with listed entities
- Financial institutions must screen against updated Annex I

**Technology Export Restrictions**:
- Enhanced scrutiny for dual-use technologies that could support surveillance
- Internet censorship tools and surveillance software exports prohibited

## 實作時程與檢查點

**Immediate Effect**: Regulation entered into force upon publication (29 January 2026)

**Member State Actions**:
- Implement asset freezes immediately
- Update national enforcement databases
- Notify financial institutions and technology exporters
- Enforce transaction prohibitions

**Ongoing Compliance**:
- Continuous transaction screening by financial institutions
- Export control authorities monitor technology transfers
- National competent authorities enforce asset freezes

## 風險與注意事項

**Novel Entity Types**:
- **Media regulatory bodies** (SATRA, WGDICC) - first time media censorship apparatus explicitly targeted
- **Software companies** (Douran, Yaftar) - dual-use technology providers
- **Research institutes** (Masaf) - disinformation infrastructure support

**Accountability Basis**:
- **Protest suppression**: Roles in coordinating crackdowns on demonstrations
- **Arbitrary detention**: Oversight of politically motivated arrests
- **Torture**: Judicial and prosecutorial roles in torture cases
- **Internet censorship**: Institutional responsibility for blocking access and surveillance

**Compliance Challenges**:
- Surveillance entities may operate through international partnerships
- Software companies may have legitimate commercial applications
- Research institutes may publish openly while supporting state activities
- Media regulatory bodies interface with international broadcasting

**Technology Transfer Risks**:
- Dual-use surveillance and censorship tools
- Internet filtering and monitoring systems
- Biometric identification technologies
- Data analytics platforms for population monitoring

## 與其他法規的關聯

- **Regulation (EU) No 359/2011**: Primary Iran human rights sanctions framework
- **EU Dual-Use Regulation (EU) 2021/821**: Controls on surveillance technologies
- **EU Global Human Rights Sanctions Regime**: Complementary human rights framework
- **Digital Services Act**: EU standards on content moderation (contrasts with Iranian censorship)
