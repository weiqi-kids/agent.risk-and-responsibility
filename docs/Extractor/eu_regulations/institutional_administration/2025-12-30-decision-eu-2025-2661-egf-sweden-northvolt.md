## L1 — Rule Signal
- **rule_type**: new
- **issuing_body**: European Parliament and Council
- **document_id**: CELEX:32025D2661
- **status**: final
- **document_type**: decision
- **binding_force**: directly_applicable
- **effective_date**: 2025-12-30 (date of publication)

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: European Union, Sweden, redundant workers and ceased self-employed persons affected by Northvolt AB restructuring
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Mobilizes EUR 8,526,322 from European Globalisation Adjustment Fund to support workers displaced by Northvolt AB restructuring and associated supplier disruptions

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Labor market disruption (mass redundancies)
- Industrial restructuring (automotive/battery sector)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Provision of EUR 8,526,322 in commitment and payment appropriations for 2025 general budget
  - Support for redundant workers and ceased self-employed persons affected by Northvolt AB restructuring
  - Compliance with maximum annual EGF cap of EUR 30 million (2018 prices)
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Application documentation submitted by Sweden on 16 June 2025
  - Evidence of worker displacements at Northvolt AB, subsidiaries, and 13 associated suppliers and downstream producers
  - Compliance with Regulation (EU) 2021/691 Article 15(1)
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
The EGF aims to demonstrate solidarity and promote decent and sustainable employment by supporting redundant workers and ceased self-employed persons during major restructuring events. This decision entered into force on the date of publication and is applicable from 17 December 2025. Signed by European Parliament President R. Metsola and Council President M. Bjerre in Strasbourg.
