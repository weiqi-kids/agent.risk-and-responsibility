## What Changed

This implementing decision expands the list of sanctioned persons and entities under the EU's Common Foreign and Security Policy restrictive measures framework targeting Iran.

**Article 1** adds 15 natural persons and 6 legal entities to Annex to Decision 2011/235/CFSP.

**Article 2** establishes immediate entry into force upon publication.

### Newly Listed Persons (15)
High-ranking Iranian government and security officials designated for serious human rights violations:
- Ministers and prosecutors involved in suppression of protests
- Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) commanders at multiple levels
- Revolutionary court judges conducting arbitrary trials
- Police and security force leadership responsible for arrests and torture

### Newly Listed Entities (6)
Iranian organizations involved in censorship, surveillance, and disinformation:
- **Iranian Audio-Visual Media Regulatory Authority (SATRA)** - state media censorship body
- **Seraj Cyberspace Organization** - internet filtering and monitoring
- **Working Group for Determining Instances of Criminal Content** - content censorship coordination
- **Yaftar Software Company** - surveillance technology provider
- **Douran Software** - digital monitoring systems
- **Masaf Institute** - cyber operations and disinformation campaigns

## Who Must Act

**EU Member States**: Implement asset freezes and travel bans against listed persons/entities immediately upon publication.

**Financial institutions in EU**: Screen transactions and freeze assets of designated individuals and organizations.

**Border authorities**: Enforce travel bans on listed natural persons.

**Competent authorities**: Monitor compliance with restrictive measures and report violations.

## Shift in Risk/Responsibility

**Shift Type**: expanded

**What responsibility is transferred/created**:
- Financial institutions must expand sanctions screening to include 21 new entries (15 persons + 6 entities)
- Border control authorities must update watchlists with new travel ban designations
- Compliance officers face increased due diligence requirements for Iran-related transactions

**Enforcement Signal**: mandatory

**Justification cited**:
Documented human rights violations during December 2025 nationwide demonstrations in Iran, including:
- Suppression of protests through excessive force
- Arbitrary detention and imprisonment
- Torture and ill-treatment of detainees
- Restriction of internet freedoms and access to information
- Censorship of independent media

The decision explicitly targets individuals and entities responsible for "serious human rights violations" in the context of Iranian government crackdown on dissent.
