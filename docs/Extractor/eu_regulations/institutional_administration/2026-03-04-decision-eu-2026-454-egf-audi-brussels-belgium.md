## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Belgium (applicant Member State), Audi Brussels S.A., five supplier/downstream producer companies, displaced workers and self-employed persons, European Commission
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Mobilises EUR 7,527,625 from the European Globalisation Adjustment Fund for Displaced Workers to support Belgian workers made redundant in the Audi Brussels restructuring and supply chain.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Labour market disruption and industrial restructuring
- EU social safety net and solidarity mechanisms
- Supply chain employment impact (automotive sector)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - EUR 7,527,625 allocated in both commitment and payment appropriations to the 2026 EU general budget
  - Belgium must use funds to support active labour market measures helping redundant workers and self-employed persons return to sustainable employment
  - Application EGF/2025/006 BE/Audi covers workers displaced at Audi Brussels S.A. and five suppliers/downstream producers
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Belgium submitted formal EGF application demonstrating threshold of displaced workers
  - Displacement linked to major structural changes in world trade patterns or global financial crisis (standard EGF eligibility)
  - European Parliament and Council joint approval required for EGF mobilisation decisions
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This decision follows the closure of Audi Brussels, a significant industrial event in the Belgian automotive sector. EGF mobilisation decisions are joint acts of the European Parliament and Council, reflecting the EU's solidarity mechanism for large-scale redundancies.
