## Level 4: Obligation, Evidence & Enforcement

### New Obligations
- **European Parliament President**: Forward decision and accompanying resolution to the Agency, Council, Commission, Court of Auditors; arrange publication in Official Journal
- **EU Agency for Asylum**: Implement any observations in accompanying resolution (not provided but referenced); maintain corrective measures that resolved earlier Parliamentary concerns
- **Executive Director**: Continue compliance with financial management standards that secured discharge approval

### Evidence Requirements
- Court of Auditors' annual report on EU agencies for 2023
- Statement of assurance on account reliability and transaction legality
- Council recommendation 05755/2025 (supporting discharge)
- Documentation of corrective actions implemented between May 2025 (postponement) and October 2025 (approval)
- EU Agency for Asylum annual accounts for financial year 2023
- Compliance evidence with Regulation (EU) 2021/2303 (Agency establishment) Article 55 and Regulation (EU, Euratom) 2018/1046 (financial rules) Article 70

### Enforcement Signal
- **Resolution of earlier concerns**: Discharge approval follows postponement (7 May 2025), indicating Agency successfully addressed Parliamentary concerns within 5-month corrective period
- **Positive accountability signal**: Approval demonstrates effective EU agency financial management governance when issues are identified and resolved
- **Ongoing monitoring**: Court of Auditors will continue annual audit cycles; Parliamentary discharge procedure repeats annually
- **No penalties**: Discharge approval closes 2023 accountability cycle without sanctions
