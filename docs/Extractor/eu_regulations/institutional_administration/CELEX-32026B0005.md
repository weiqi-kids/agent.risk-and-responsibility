## What Changed

This document constitutes the definitive adoption of the third amending budget for the EU's 2025 financial year, completing the Article 314 TFEU procedure following Council approval (17 November 2025) and European Parliament approval (26 November 2025).

### Total Budget Adjustments

**Total Revenue**: €161,162,528,615

**Own Resources Adjustments**:
- Customs duties: €22,178,600,000 (+€1,300,000,000 increase from original budget)
- VAT-based resource: €23,814,511,650
- GNI-based resource: €101,480,252,237 (–€46,913,911 reduction)
- Plastic packaging waste resource: €6,848,152,160
- Miscellaneous revenue: €5,496,479,429

### Institutional Expenditure Adjustments

**European Parliament**: €2,527,491,841 (–€5,203,267 reduction)
**Council & European Council**: €714,268,858 (–€1,680,000 reduction)
**European Commission**: Major allocations across 15 policy titles with various adjustments

### Key Policy Areas (Commission)

The amendment adjusts allocations across:
- European Strategic Investments (Title 02)
- Single Market (Title 03)
- Regional Development & Cohesion (Title 05)
- Recovery & Resilience (Title 06)
- Social Cohesion & Values (Title 07)
- Agriculture & Maritime Policy (Title 08)
- Environment & Climate Action (Title 09)
- Migration (Title 10)
- Border Management (Title 11)

## Who Must Act

**EU Member States**:
- Contribute own resources according to adjusted formulas (VAT-based at 0.30%, GNI-based at 0.5472%)
- Collect and transfer customs duties (75% net to EU budget after collection costs)
- Pay plastic packaging waste levies based on non-recycled waste tonnage

**European Parliament**: Approved amendment 26 November 2025

**Council of the European Union**: Adopted position 17 November 2025

**European Commission**:
- Proposed draft amending budget 3 October 2025
- Execute budget adjustments across policy areas

**National treasuries**: Transfer adjusted contributions to EU budget

## Shift in Risk/Responsibility

**Shift Type**: clarified

**What responsibility is transferred/created**:
- Member States face adjusted financial obligations for final months of 2025 fiscal year
- GNI-based contribution reduced by €46.9M (redistributed among Member States)
- Customs duties collection increased by €1.3B (25% retained by Member States as collection costs)
- Lump-sum reductions maintained for Denmark, Germany, Netherlands, Austria, Sweden per established formulas
- Example: Germany's total contribution €33,840,491,852 (customs €4.2B + VAT €5.6B + GNI €25.0B)

**Enforcement Signal**: mandatory

**Justification cited**:
President of European Parliament declared "the procedure under Article 314 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union is complete" following sequential approvals by Commission, Council, and Parliament.

The amendment adjusts 2025 budget allocations to reflect:
- Updated customs revenue forecasts (+€1.3B)
- Refined GNI-based contribution calculations (–€46.9M)
- Institutional spending adjustments (Parliament –€5.2M, Council –€1.7M)
