## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU Member States, competent national authorities, financial institutions, border control authorities
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Amends the CFSP sanctions framework by adding four RSF commanders responsible for violence and human rights abuses in Darfur, Sudan, implementing UNSC designations.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Sanctions compliance and enforcement (CFSP)
- International humanitarian law violations
- Geopolitical risk (Sudan/Darfur conflict)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Member States must implement CFSP restrictive measures against four newly designated RSF commanders: Gedo Hamdan Ahmed (Abu Nashuk), Abdul Rahim Hamdan Dagalo, Abdullah Idris Al-Fateh (Abu Lulu), Tijani Ibrahim Moussa Mohamed (Al Zeir Salem)
  - Measures operate under Decision 2014/450/CFSP and UNSC Resolution 1591(2005)
  - Asset freeze and travel restrictions apply immediately
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Basis: UN Security Council Resolution 1591 (2005) and UNSC Committee designations of 24 February 2026
  - Documented atrocities: mass killings of civilians, ethnically targeted executions in El Fasher, 26 October 2025
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This CFSP Decision is the Common Foreign and Security Policy counterpart to Council Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/531 (CELEX:32026R0531), published on the same date and targeting the same four individuals. Together they give full legal effect to the UN sanctions designations.
