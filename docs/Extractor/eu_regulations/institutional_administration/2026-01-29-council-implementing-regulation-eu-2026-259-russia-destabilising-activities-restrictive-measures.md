## L2: 權責轉移分析

**affected_roles**:
- EU Member States (sanctions enforcement)
- Listed individuals (6 Russian media personalities)
- Financial institutions (asset freeze compliance)
- Media organizations (awareness of sanctioned individuals)

**shift_type**: Authority → Enforcement

**shift_summary**: Regulation amends Annex I to Regulation (EU) 2024/2642 by adding six Russian media personalities to the restrictive measures list for their role in supporting Russian government actions that undermine democracy and spread disinformation related to the Ukraine conflict.

---

## L3: Risk Domains

**Primary Domains**:
- Information manipulation and disinformation
- State propaganda operations
- Democracy undermining activities
- Media-facilitated influence operations

**Listed Individuals**:
1. Dmitry Viktorovich Guberniev (TV presenter, journalist, sports commentator)
2. Ekaterina Sergeevna Andreeva (television news anchor)
3. Maria Eduardovna Sittel (television news presenter)
4. Pavel Aleksandrovich Zarubin (journalist, Putin interviewer)
5. Roman Vasilyevich Chumakov (actor and singer)
6. Sergey Vladimirovich Polunin (ballet dancer, former academy rector)

**Designation Rationale**: Individuals designated as responsible for "actions and policies attributable to the Government of the Russian Federation which undermine or threaten democracy" through information manipulation and supporting violent conflict in Ukraine.

---

## L4: 操作衝擊

### new_obligations

**For EU Member States**:
- Implement immediate asset freezes on listed individuals
- Enforce travel bans and visa restrictions
- Monitor and block financial transactions involving listed persons
- Report compliance to EU authorities

**For Financial Institutions**:
- Screen transactions against updated sanctions list
- Freeze assets of listed individuals immediately
- Report attempted transactions by listed persons
- Maintain enhanced monitoring of Russia-related media accounts

**For Media Organizations**:
- Awareness that engagement with listed individuals may trigger sanctions compliance reviews
- Exercise caution in business dealings with sanctioned media personalities

### evidence_requirements

- Identity verification documentation for listed individuals
- Transaction screening records
- Asset freeze implementation records
- Compliance audit trails

### enforcement_signal

**Enforcement Mechanism**: Enforcement operates through EU member state compliance with restrictive measures provisions under Regulation (EU) 2024/2642.

**Enforcement Authority**: Member State competent authorities responsible for sanctions implementation

**Penalties**: Administrative and criminal penalties as specified under national sanctions implementation laws

**Compliance Timeline**: Immediate (upon entry into force on 2026-01-29)

**Risk Level**: High - violations may result in significant fines and potential criminal prosecution

## Notes

**Rationale**: Targets Russian media personalities actively promoting Russian government narratives that undermine democratic values and support violent conflict in Ukraine through their public platforms and influence.

**Legal Basis**: Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union; Council Regulation (EU) 2024/2642 (Article 13(1))

**Related Legislation**: Regulation (EU) 2024/2642 concerning restrictive measures in view of Russia's destabilising activities

**Context**: Part of broader EU sanctions strategy addressing information manipulation and propaganda operations supporting Russia's actions in Ukraine.
