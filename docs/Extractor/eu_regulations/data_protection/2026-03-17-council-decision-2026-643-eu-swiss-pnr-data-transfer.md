## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Air carriers operating EU-Switzerland routes, EU member states' law enforcement authorities (except Denmark), Swiss competent authorities, Europol, Eurojust, European Data Protection Supervisor
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: The EU authorises signing a bilateral agreement with Switzerland enabling air carriers to transfer Passenger Name Record (PNR) data to Swiss competent authorities for counter-terrorism and serious crime prevention, with fundamental rights safeguards aligned to the EU Charter.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Cross-border data transfer, passenger data privacy, law enforcement data sharing

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Air carriers on EU-Switzerland routes must transfer PNR data under the agreement framework
  - Competent authorities must implement safeguards respecting EU Charter fundamental rights on privacy and personal data
  - Ireland (participating despite protocol exemption) and member states must apply the agreement's data protection provisions
  - European Data Protection Supervisor consultation requirements fulfilled prior to adoption
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Data transfer documentation from air carriers
  - Evidence of fundamental rights safeguard implementation
  - EDPS consultation records
  - Compliance reports from national competent authorities
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
