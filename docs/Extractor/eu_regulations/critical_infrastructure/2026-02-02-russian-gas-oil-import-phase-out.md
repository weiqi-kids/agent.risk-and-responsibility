## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Energy importers, customs authorities, Member States, national energy regulators, competent authorities, European Commission
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes stepwise prohibition on Russian natural gas imports (pipeline gas by June 2026, LNG by April 2026) with authorization requirements and diversification planning obligations for Member States

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Energy security and independence
- Supply chain resilience
- Geopolitical risk management
- Economic sanctions enforcement
- Critical infrastructure dependency

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Importers must obtain prior authorization for gas imports and provide detailed contract documentation
  - Prohibition on Russian pipeline gas from 17 June 2026 (or 1 November 2027 for certain Member States meeting storage conditions)
  - Prohibition on Russian LNG from 25 April 2026 (or 1 January 2027 for long-term contracts)
  - Member States must prepare national diversification plans by 1 March 2026
  - Customs authorities must enforce import prohibitions and verify country of production
  - Commission may grant emergency exemptions under specified conditions
  - Member States must notify penalties provisions by 4 February 2028
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Import authorization documentation
  - Contract documentation establishing country of production
  - National diversification plans
  - Storage capacity verification for extended transition periods
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This regulation amends Regulation (EU) 2017/1938 on gas supply security. Long-term contracts receive extended exemptions (up to 30 September 2027), while short-term contracts have earlier deadlines. The regulation establishes differentiated transition timelines based on Member State energy dependency levels.
