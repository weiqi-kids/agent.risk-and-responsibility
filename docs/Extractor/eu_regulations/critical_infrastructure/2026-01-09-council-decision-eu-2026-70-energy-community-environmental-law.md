## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: European Commission, Energy Community Contracting Parties, Member States, energy infrastructure operators, protected area authorities
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Authorizes Commission to propose amendments expanding Energy Community Treaty environmental acquis to incorporate biodiversity and water protection directives

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Environmental risk (biodiversity and water protection)
- Energy security (renewable infrastructure compliance)
- Critical infrastructure (energy-water-ecosystem interdependencies)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Integration of Wild Birds Directive 2009/147/EC provisions (Article 2, 4(4), 5, 9) within five years
  - Integration of Habitats Directive 92/43/EEC provisions (Article 6(2)-(4), 12-13, 16) within five years
  - Integration of Water Framework Directive 2000/60/EC and related directives within five years
  - Prevention of water body deterioration and achievement of "good water status"
  - River basin management planning integrating energy project compliance
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Assessment of energy project impacts on protected areas and water ecosystems
  - Compliance verification with habitat protection outside designated areas
  - Species protection regime implementation for Emerald Network Areas and Ramsar sites
  - Chemical water analysis per technical specifications
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This decision balances renewable energy acceleration with biodiversity protection and water resource enhancement. Implementation addresses impacts of existing and planned energy infrastructure on protected areas and water ecosystems. The five-year implementation deadlines apply to all contracting parties of the Energy Community Treaty.
