## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member states, energy importers, gas and LNG suppliers, regulatory authorities, customs
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Prohibits Russian pipeline gas imports from 17 June 2026 and LNG from 25 April 2026, with temporary exemptions for existing contracts and mandatory national diversification plans

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Critical infrastructure
- Energy security
- Supply chain resilience
- Sanctions enforcement

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Cease Russian pipeline gas imports by 17 June 2026 (unless covered by qualifying exemption)
  - Cease Russian LNG imports by 25 April 2026 (unless covered by qualifying exemption)
  - Establish prior authorization procedures for exempted imports
  - Submit national diversification plans by 1 March 2026
  - Implement cooperation mechanisms between customs, regulatory, and energy authorities
  - Apply maximum penalties of 3.5% worldwide turnover, €40 million, or 300% transaction value
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Supply contract documentation demonstrating exemption eligibility
  - National diversification plan submissions describing alternative suppliers, renewable energy measures, and energy efficiency initiatives
  - Authorization records for exempted imports
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Entry into force: 3 February 2026. Application from 18 March 2026. Contracts concluded after 17 June 2025 receive no exemption. Long-term contract exemptions extend to September 2027 (pipeline) and January 2027 (LNG).
