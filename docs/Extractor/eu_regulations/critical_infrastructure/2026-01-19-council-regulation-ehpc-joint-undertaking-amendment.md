## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU Member States, private and public technology consortia establishing AI gigafactories, research institutions, SMEs, start-ups, scale-ups accessing computing infrastructure, the European High Performance Computing Joint Undertaking
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Extends the Joint Undertaking's mandate to support AI gigafactories and quantum technologies with specific financial commitment requirements for Member States and infrastructure networking provisions

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Critical infrastructure (high-performance computing, AI factories, quantum technologies)
- Technology governance (AI and quantum technology development)
- Financial compliance (Member States' financial commitments, Recovery and Resilience Facility fund transfers)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Member States must provide explicit financial commitments before AI gigafactory selection calls launch
  - Union contributes up to 17% of AI gigafactory capital expenditure; participating states must match this minimum
  - Member States allocating RRF funds must complete transfers by 31 August 2026 to meet RRF deadlines
  - AI gigafactories require hosting agreements governed by Union law with strategic interest protection provisions
  - Research activities up to Technology Readiness Level 5 receive 100% Union funding for quantum technologies
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Documentation of Member State financial commitments for AI gigafactories
  - Hosting agreements governed by Union law
  - RRF fund transfer completion records by 31 August 2026
  - Quantum Technologies Advisory Group strategy development records
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
