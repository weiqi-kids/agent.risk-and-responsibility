## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: European Commission (designated negotiator), Ukraine (third-country participant), Council Working Party on Space (oversight), EU Member States
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Authorises the European Commission to open negotiations for Ukraine to participate in GOVSATCOM (governmental satellite communications) and the EU Secure Connectivity Programme, extending critical space infrastructure access to a third country under specific security and financial conditions.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Space-based critical infrastructure security
- Governmental satellite communications (GOVSATCOM)
- Secure connectivity and classified information protection
- EU-third country security cooperation

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - European Commission authorised to negotiate and must report to Council Working Party on Space
  - Ukraine (prospectively) must designate competent authorities for both programmes
  - Ukraine must pay financial contributions (assigned revenues to EU budget)
  - Ukraine must adopt secure connectivity regulatory measures equivalent to EU standards
  - Ukraine must accept OLAF and Court of Auditors inspections
  - Ukraine must recognise EU ownership of secure connectivity assets
  - Classified information handling must comply with existing EU-Ukraine security agreements
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Negotiating mandate issued under TFEU Art. 189(2) in conjunction with Arts. 218(3) and (4)
  - Commission recommendations reviewed prior to authorisation
  - Progress reports to Council Working Party on Space required during negotiations
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
