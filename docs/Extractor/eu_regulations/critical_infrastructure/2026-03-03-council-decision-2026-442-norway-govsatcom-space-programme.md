## L1 — Rule Signal
- **rule_type**: new

- **document_id**: CELEX:32026D0442
- **status**: final
- **document_type**: decision
- **binding_force**: directly_applicable
- **effective_date**: 2026-02-17 (date of adoption)

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Kingdom of Norway, European Union, EU Space Programme agencies, users of GOVSATCOM services, entities accessing the Union Secure Connectivity Programme
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Authorises signing of the EU-Norway Agreement establishing rules for Norway's participation in GOVSATCOM and the Union Secure Connectivity Programme, expanding access to EU strategic space infrastructure.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Critical space infrastructure security
- Governmental satellite communications (GOVSATCOM)
- European defence and strategic autonomy
- Third-country access to EU secure connectivity services

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Norway is authorised to participate in the GOVSATCOM component of the Union Space Programme
  - Norway gains access to GOVSATCOM services and governmental services of the Union Secure Connectivity Programme
  - Participation subject to rules laid down in the bilateral Agreement (to be formally signed following this decision)
  - European Commission authorised to sign the Agreement on behalf of the EU
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Agreement must lay down rules for participation and service access consistent with EU Space Strategy objectives
  - Norway must comply with security and interoperability requirements for GOVSATCOM access
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
GOVSATCOM provides secure, reliable satellite communication services to EU governmental users including defence, civil protection, and border management. Extending access to Norway (an EEA member and NATO ally) strengthens European geopolitical cooperation. The decision authorises signing only; formal ratification and entry into force will follow a subsequent Council act.
