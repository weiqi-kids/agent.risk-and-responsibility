## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Member states, ADR entities, traders, consumers, ADR contact points
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Expands ADR to cover domestic, cross-border, and third-country trader disputes following discontinuation of European Online Dispute Resolution Platform, with mandatory 20-working-day response requirements and enhanced accessibility provisions

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Digital market governance
- Consumer protection
- Cross-border trade

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Establish measures promoting trader and consumer participation in ADR procedures
  - Ensure ADR entities handle domestic, cross-border, and third-country trader disputes
  - Designate ADR contact points for cross-border assistance
  - Require traders to respond to ADR participation invitations within 20 working days (extendable to 30 days)
  - Ensure automated decision-making transparency
  - Implement case bundling procedures
  - Apply to disputes with third-country traders directing activities toward Member States
  - Implement measures by 20 March 2028
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - ADR participation documentation
  - Response time compliance records
  - Cross-border coordination documentation
  - Accessibility compliance records
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Amends Directive 2013/11/EU and Directives (EU) 2015/2302, (EU) 2019/2161, (EU) 2020/1828. Application from 20 September 2028. Covers out-of-court dispute resolution for sales or service contracts including digital content/services.
