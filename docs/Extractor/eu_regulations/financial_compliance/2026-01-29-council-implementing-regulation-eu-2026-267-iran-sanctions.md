## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU Member States, Financial institutions, Asset controllers, Customs authorities, Iranian government officials, Iranian entities subject to sanctions
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Adds 15 individuals and 6 entities to Iran sanctions list for human rights violations including arbitrary detention of foreign nationals and use of violence against protesters

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Sanctions and Export Control: Implementation of restrictive measures against Iranian persons and entities
- Human Rights: Response to arbitrary detention and violence against protesters
- Financial Compliance: Asset freezing requirements
- Cross-border Transactions: Restrictions on dealings with listed persons/entities

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Asset freezing for 15 newly listed Iranian officials (including Interior Minister, Prosecutor General, Police Commander)
  - Asset freezing for 6 newly listed entities (including media regulators, cyber organizations, software companies)
  - Prohibition on making funds or economic resources available to listed persons/entities
  - Due diligence requirements to identify and block transactions involving listed persons/entities
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Documentation of asset freezing implementation
  - Transaction screening records
  - Compliance reports to competent authorities
  - Records of denied transactions involving listed persons/entities
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This implementing regulation amends Regulation (EU) No 359/2011. The sanctions target Iranian officials and entities involved in human rights violations, specifically arbitrary detention of foreign citizens and use of violence against protesters. All EU Member States must implement asset freezes immediately.
