## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Financial institutions, payment service providers, asset managers, member state competent authorities, cryptocurrency exchanges, financial intermediaries
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Expands financial sanctions list by adding 15 individuals and 6 entities subject to asset freeze measures under Iran sanctions regime

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial sanctions compliance
- Anti-money laundering (AML)
- Know Your Customer (KYC) obligations
- Cross-border payment controls
- Asset management compliance

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Freeze all funds and economic resources of 15 newly listed Iranian officials
  - Freeze assets of 6 newly listed Iranian entities
  - Prohibit making funds or economic resources available to listed persons/entities
  - Screen all transactions against updated sanctions list
  - Report any frozen assets to competent authorities
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Customer due diligence records against sanctions list
  - Transaction screening logs showing compliance checks
  - Frozen asset reports to national competent authorities
  - Documentation of prohibited transaction rejections
  - Beneficial ownership verification for entities connected to listed persons
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This regulation is directly applicable in all member states without requiring national transposition. Financial institutions must immediately update their sanctions screening systems to include the 15 individuals (including Iranian Minister of Interior, Prosecutor General, IRGC commanders, judges, police commanders, cyberspace officials) and 6 entities (including SATRA, Seraj Cyberspace Organization, Working Group for Determining Instances of Criminal Content, Yaftar Pazhohan, Douran Software Technologies, Masaf Institute). Violations may result in criminal penalties under national implementing legislation.
