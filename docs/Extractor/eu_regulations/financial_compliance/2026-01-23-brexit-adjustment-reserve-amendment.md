## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: European Commission, Member States, Recovery and Resilience Facility
- **shift_type**: restricted
- **shift_summary**: Reduces Brexit Adjustment Reserve by EUR 584,264,090 to redirect funds toward geopolitical emergencies while preserving existing pre-financing and transferred amounts

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial compliance
- Budget management
- Crisis response

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Reserve's maximum resources reduced by EUR 584,264,090 (2025 disbursement amount)
  - Resources already paid as pre-financing remain unaffected
  - Amounts already transferred to Recovery and Resilience Facility remain protected
  - Member States may submit reasoned requests to transfer allocations to Recovery and Resilience Facility by 1 March 2023 deadline
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Documentation of pre-financing payments
  - Records of transfers to Recovery and Resilience Facility
  - Member State reasoned requests for allocation transfers
  - Updated allocation schedules reflecting EUR 584,264,090 reduction
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Amendment responds to geopolitical emergencies (Russia's war against Ukraine, energy crisis, inflation) requiring budget redeployment across EU's 2024-2027 financial framework. Regulation modifies Article 4 and Article 4a of Regulation (EU) 2021/1755. Published 23 January 2026, enters into force 12 February 2026.
