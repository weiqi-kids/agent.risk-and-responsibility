## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU exporters and traders, manufacturers of controlled materials and equipment, dual-use goods suppliers, customs authorities, export control authorities
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Expands export prohibitions to Iran by adding components used in developing and producing unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and missiles across 10 categories

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Prohibition on exporting to Iran: energetic materials, specialized electronics, machine tools, sensors, navigation systems, and aerospace components listed in amended Annex II
  - Exporters must screen all controlled items across 10 newly defined categories
  - Customs authorities must enforce expanded export prohibitions
  - Companies must update export control compliance programs to reflect expanded list
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Export control screening records
  - Dual-use item classification documentation
  - Customs export declarations
  - Compliance program updates
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
This regulation amends Regulation (EU) 2023/1529 concerning restrictive measures in view of Iran's military support to Russia's war of aggression against Ukraine and to armed groups in the Middle East and Red Sea region. The expanded controlled items list in Annex II comprises 10 categories targeting UAV and missile development capabilities. Category assigned as financial_compliance due to focus on sanctions enforcement and export controls, though also has cybersecurity and supply_chain dimensions.
