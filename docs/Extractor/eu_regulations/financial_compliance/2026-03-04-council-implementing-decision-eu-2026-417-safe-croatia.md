## L1 — Rule Signal

- **document_id**: CELEX:32026D0417
- **status**: final

- **binding_force**: directly_applicable

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Republic of Croatia, European Commission, defence industry entities participating in common/single procurements, EU budget authority
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Makes available a maximum loan of EUR 1,700,000,000 to Croatia under the Security Action for Europe (SAFE) instrument (Regulation (EU) 2025/1106) for defence industrial investment and EU interoperability.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- European defence industrial capacity and procurement compliance
- Financial compliance (SAFE instrument loan conditionality)
- Critical infrastructure resilience (defence industrial base)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Croatia must comply with Regulation (EU) 2025/1106 conditions, including Article 16 procurement rules
  - Activities must relate to "defence products or other products for defence purposes" through common or single procurements
  - Measures must achieve: defence industry adjustment, timely availability of defence products, or interoperability across the Union
  - Pre-financing of EUR 255,000,000 available; total maximum loan EUR 1,700,000,000
  - Financial interests of the Union must be protected through loan agreements
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Croatia must demonstrate compliance with SAFE procurement conditions before loan drawdown
  - Activities must target defence products meeting SAFE eligibility criteria
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Croatia's SAFE loan allocation (EUR 1.7 billion) is significantly larger than Denmark's (EUR 46.8 million, CELEX:32026D0418), reflecting the scale of Croatia's approved defence investment programme. Both decisions implement Regulation (EU) 2025/1106 and are part of the broader EU push to rearm and strengthen its defence industrial base in 2026.
