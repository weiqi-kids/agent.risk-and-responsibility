## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: European Commission, patent right holders, licensees, manufacturers, Member states
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes Union-level compulsory licensing procedures for patented products during declared crises when voluntary agreements fail, requiring adequate remuneration, product tracking, and EU-only distribution as last resort measure

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial compliance
- Intellectual property
- Crisis management
- Supply chain security

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Commission may grant compulsory licenses when declared crisis requires and voluntary agreements unachievable
  - Licensees must pay adequate remuneration to right holders
  - Products must be identifiable with manufacturing records maintained
  - Prohibit export of licensed products (EU internal market only)
  - Provide right holders notice and opportunity to comment
  - Comply with quality and supply requirements
  - Operate as measure of last resort
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Crisis declaration documentation
  - Voluntary agreement negotiation records
  - Remuneration payment documentation
  - Manufacturing and tracking records
  - Quality compliance documentation
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Applies to patents, utility models, supplementary protection certificates, and published patent applications. Does not apply to defense-related products or affect copyright/trade secret protections. Amends Regulation (EC) No 816/2006.
