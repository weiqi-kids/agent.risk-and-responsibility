## L1 — Rule Signal

- **issuing_body**: Council of the European Union
- **document_id**: CELEX:32026D0418
- **status**: final

- **binding_force**: directly_applicable
- **effective_date**: 2026-03-04 (publication date in Official Journal)

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Kingdom of Denmark, European Commission, defence industry entities participating in common/single procurements, EU budget authority
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Makes available a maximum loan of EUR 46,796,822 to Denmark under the Security Action for Europe (SAFE) instrument (Regulation (EU) 2025/1106) to accelerate defence industrial investment and interoperability.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- European defence industrial capacity and procurement compliance
- Financial compliance (SAFE instrument loan conditionality)
- Critical infrastructure resilience (defence industrial base)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Denmark must comply with Article 16 of Regulation (EU) 2025/1106 regarding procurement rules
  - Activities must be executed through common or single procurements related to defence products
  - Measures must serve objectives: accelerating defence industry adjustment, improving product availability, or ensuring EU-wide interoperability
  - Pre-financing of EUR 7,019,523.30 available; total maximum loan EUR 46,796,822
  - Financial interests of the Union must be protected through loan agreements
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Denmark must demonstrate compliance with SAFE procurement conditions before drawdown
  - Activities must target "defence products or other products for defence purposes"
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
The SAFE instrument (Regulation (EU) 2025/1106) is part of the EU's effort to strengthen European defence industrial capacity. This decision is one of a series of Member State-specific implementing decisions activating SAFE loans; a parallel decision (CELEX:32026D0417) covers Croatia.
