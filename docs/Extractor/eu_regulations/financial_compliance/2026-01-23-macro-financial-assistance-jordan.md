## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, European Commission, European External Action Service, European Anti-Fraud Office (OLAF), Court of Auditors, Jordan Central Bank, Jordan Ministry of Finance
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Establishes EUR 500 million macro-financial assistance program requiring Jordan to implement economic reforms and maintain democratic standards while granting Commission monitoring and anti-fraud investigation powers

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial compliance
- Governance and transparency
- International cooperation
- Anti-fraud measures

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Jordan must respect effective democratic mechanisms and rule of law
  - Jordan must implement economic policy measures focusing on structural reforms and sound public finances
  - Jordan must maintain continuous satisfactory track record with IMF-supported policy programme
  - Jordan must take appropriate measures against fraud, corruption, and irregularities
  - Jordan must permit investigations by OLAF and audits by Court of Auditors
  - Commission must negotiate memorandum of understanding with Jordanian authorities
  - Commission must conduct operational assessment of Jordan's financial arrangements
  - Commission must assess precondition fulfillment and IMF programme compliance before disbursements
  - Commission must report annually by 30 June on implementation progress
  - Commission must conduct ex post evaluation within two years after availability period expires
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Signed memorandum of understanding detailing policy conditions
  - Loan agreement specifying financial terms and availability period
  - Documentation of satisfactory IMF programme implementation
  - Anti-fraud and anti-corruption compliance records
  - OLAF investigation access permissions
  - Court of Auditors audit permissions
  - Annual monitoring reports covering economic and fiscal performance
  - Ex post evaluation report
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
EUR 500 million assistance entirely in loan form, available for 2.5 years from memorandum of understanding entry into force, disbursed in three instalments with minimum three-month intervals. Commission may reduce, suspend, or cancel assistance if Jordan's financing needs decrease fundamentally or conditions aren't met.
