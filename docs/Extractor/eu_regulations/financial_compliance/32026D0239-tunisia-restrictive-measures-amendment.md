# Council Decision (CFSP) 2026/239 amending Decision 2011/72/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against certain persons and entities in Tunisia

## Metadata
- **CELEX Number**: 32026D0239
- **Document Type**: decision
- **Binding Force**: directly_applicable
- **Date of Adoption**: 2026-01-29
- **Date of Entry into Force**: 2026-01-31 (day following publication)
- **Publication Reference**: OJ L, 2026/239, 30.1.2026
- **Legal Basis**: Treaty on European Union, Article 29
- **Amends**: Decision 2011/72/CFSP

## Objective
Extend and update EU restrictive measures targeting individuals and entities associated with misappropriation of Tunisian state funds, reflecting evolving judicial proceedings and delisting three individuals following completion of legal processes.

## Key Provisions

### Extension of Restrictive Measures
Sanctions measures extended until **31 January 2027**, replacing previous expiration date of 31 January 2026.

### Delistings
**Three individuals removed from Part A of restrictive measures list:**
1. Lilia Bent Noureddine Ben Ahmed Nacef
2. Mehdi Ben Ridha Ben Mohamed Ben Gaied
3. Ghazoua Bent Zine El Abidine Ben Haj Hamda Ben Ali

**Legal basis for delisting**: Not specified in decision text (likely completion of judicial proceedings or insufficient grounds for continued listing).

### Updated Legal Protections Information
Information regarding rights of defence and effective judicial protection under Tunisian law amended for **24 persons**, reflecting ongoing investigations and recent judicial proceedings.

## Risk & Responsibility Impact

### Shift Type
- clarified
- restricted (for delisted individuals)

### Responsibility Changes

1. **Member States**:
   - Update national sanctions lists by removing three delisted individuals
   - Unfreeze assets and lift restrictions on delisted persons
   - Maintain restrictions on remaining listed individuals until 2027-01-31
   - Monitor ongoing judicial proceedings in Tunisia
   - Ensure rights of defence provisions are communicated to listed persons

2. **Delisted Individuals (3)**:
   - Asset freezes lifted
   - Travel restrictions removed
   - Access to frozen funds restored

3. **Listed Persons (24 with updated information)**:
   - Continued asset freeze obligations
   - Updated information on judicial proceedings
   - Enhanced transparency on rights of defence and judicial remedies under Tunisian law

4. **EU Institutions**:
   - Commission and EEAS monitor Tunisian judicial proceedings
   - Council reviews sanctions list at least annually
   - High Representative proposes amendments based on new information

### Enforcement Signal
- mandatory

### Sectors Affected
- Asset management and banking
- International travel and border control
- Diplomatic relations
- Judicial cooperation

### Jurisdictional Applicability
**Member States Bound**: All EU Member States (CFSP decisions bind all under TEU Article 29)
**EEA Relevance**: Limited (CFSP measures generally not directly applicable to EEA/EFTA states)
**Third Country Effect**: Extraterritorial implications for non-EU financial institutions holding assets of listed persons; third-country cooperation in travel restrictions

## Key Obligations

### Member State Obligations
1. Remove three delisted individuals from national sanctions lists
2. Unfreeze assets and lift travel bans for delisted persons
3. Update information for 24 individuals with amended legal status
4. Maintain asset freezes and travel restrictions for remaining listed persons until 2027-01-31
5. Ensure listed persons receive updated information on rights of defence

### Financial Institution Obligations
1. Unfreeze accounts and assets held by three delisted individuals
2. Process release of frozen funds in accordance with national procedures
3. Continue screening transactions against updated sanctions list
4. Report any attempted transactions by remaining listed persons

### Listed Person Rights
1. Access to information on reasons for listing
2. Right to submit request for delisting to Council
3. Access to effective judicial remedies (EU Court of Justice)
4. Right to be heard regarding continued listing

## Implementation Requirements

### Timeline
- **Entry into Force**: 2026-01-31 (day following publication)
- **Sanctions Expiry**: 2027-01-31 (unless further extended)
- **Implementation**: Member States must immediately update national lists and unfreeze assets of delisted persons

### Implementation Measures Required
1. **National level**: Update domestic sanctions registers; notify financial institutions of delistings; process asset release requests
2. **EU level**: Update EU consolidated sanctions list; notify third countries and international partners
3. **Individual level**: Notify delisted persons of sanctions removal; provide updated information to persons with amended status

## Compliance Challenges
1. **Asset release procedures**: Complexity in identifying and unfreezing assets held across multiple jurisdictions
2. **Judicial review risk**: Listed persons may challenge continued restrictions through EU courts
3. **Information updates**: Ensuring 24 persons with amended information receive adequate notification
4. **Coordination with Tunisia**: Dependency on Tunisian judicial proceedings and information sharing
5. **Third-country cooperation**: Non-EU jurisdictions may not immediately recognize delistings

## Related Legal Instruments
- Decision 2011/72/CFSP concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Tunisia (parent decision)
- Treaty on European Union (TEU) Article 29 (CFSP legal basis)
- Council Implementing Regulation on restrictive measures against Tunisia (parallel implementing instrument - CELEX number not specified)

## Cross-References to Other Documents
This Decision operates within the EU's response to the Arab Spring and Tunisian political transition, targeting individuals associated with the Ben Ali regime accused of misappropriating state funds during 2011 uprising.
