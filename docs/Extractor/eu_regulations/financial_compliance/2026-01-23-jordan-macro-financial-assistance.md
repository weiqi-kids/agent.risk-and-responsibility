## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, European Commission, Jordan's Central Bank, Jordan's Ministry of Finance
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: Provides maximum EUR 500 million in loans to Jordan over 2.5 years for balance-of-payments financing, conditional on democratic mechanisms, rule of law, human rights, and economic policy conditions per Memorandum of Understanding

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial compliance
- Sovereign risk management
- Human rights compliance
- Economic policy

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Maintain effective democratic mechanisms including multi-party parliamentary system
  - Guarantee rule of law and respect for human rights
  - Implement economic policy conditions per Memorandum of Understanding
  - Focus on public finance management efficiency, transparency, and structural reforms
  - Release funds in three installments with minimum three-month intervals
  - Disburse funds through Central Bank to Ministry of Finance
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Memorandum of Understanding documentation
  - Democratic governance verification
  - Economic policy compliance reports
  - Disbursement records
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes
Coordinated with IMF Extended Fund Facility support. Addresses economic impacts from regional instability (Syria conflict, Israel-Gaza situation), security disruptions, and unemployment. Emphasizes sustainable growth, fiscal consolidation, and fair trade within EU-Jordan Association Agreement framework.
