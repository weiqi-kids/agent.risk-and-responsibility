## L1 — Rule Signal

- **status**: final

- **binding_force**: directly_applicable
- **effective_date**: contingent on Article 103(1) EEA Agreement notifications

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EFTA States, competent authorities, resolution authorities, central counterparties operating in the EEA, financial regulators, EFTA Surveillance Authority, national central banks (excluding Liechtenstein for certain provisions)
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: Incorporates Regulation (EU) 2021/23 and three delegated regulations (2023/450, 2023/451, 2023/840) on central counterparty recovery and resolution into the EEA Agreement framework

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Financial compliance (CCP recovery and resolution mechanisms)
- Critical infrastructure (financial market infrastructure)
- Supply chain responsibility (interconnected financial systems)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - EFTA States must adopt EU position on CCP recovery and resolution framework within EEA Joint Committee
  - Resolution authorities must implement recovery and resolution mechanisms for central counterparties
  - Central counterparties must comply with recovery planning and resolution frameworks
  - Competent authorities must supervise CCP compliance with recovery requirements
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Recovery plans from central counterparties
  - Resolution authority assessment documentation
  - Supervisory reports from competent authorities
  - Notification records under Article 103(1) EEA Agreement
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

WebFetch successful. This Decision establishes the EU's negotiating position for incorporating the CCP recovery and resolution framework into the EEA Agreement, affecting Annexes IX (Financial services), XII (Free movement of capital), and XXII (Company law).
