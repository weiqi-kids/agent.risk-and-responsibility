## L1 — Rule Signal

- **document_type**: Regulation
- **binding_force**: directly_applicable

- **regulation_type**: Implementing Regulation
- **legal_basis**: Regulation (EU) 2024/386
- **effective_date**: 2026-01-17 (day following publication)
- **signal_strength**: Mandatory — directly applicable in all Member States
- **amendment_nature**: Updates sanctions list by adding one individual and removing one deceased individual

## L2 — Responsibility Structure

- **affected_roles**: Financial institutions (banks, investment firms, payment service providers), compliance officers, sanctions screening teams, asset managers
- **shift_type**: clarified
- **shift_summary**: Financial institutions must immediately update sanctions screening systems to freeze assets of newly listed individual (Sudan-based Hamas financier) and remove deceased individual from watchlists

## L3 — Risk Domains

- **primary_risk**: Terrorist financing, sanctions evasion
- **secondary_risk**: Reputational risk from non-compliance, regulatory penalties
- **cross_border_dimension**: EU-wide sanctions with extraterritorial implications for entities dealing with EU financial system

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence

- **new_obligations**:
  - Update sanctions screening databases to include Abdelbasit Elhassan Mohamed Khair Hamza (Sudan-based Hamas financier managing investment companies)
  - Remove Ismail Barhoum (deceased) from active sanctions lists
  - Freeze assets and prohibit making funds or economic resources available to listed individual
  - Report any frozen assets or attempted transactions to competent authorities

- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Timestamped sanctions list updates in screening systems
  - Documentation of asset freeze actions (if applicable)
  - Transaction monitoring logs showing blocked attempts
  - Internal compliance records demonstrating timely implementation

- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory

## Notes

- This regulation implements targeted financial sanctions against individuals supporting Hamas and Palestinian Islamic Jihad violent activities
- The listed individual (Hamza) is identified as managing investment companies in Sudan that channel funds to Hamas
- Removal of deceased individual (Barhoum) reflects standard sanctions list maintenance
- Financial institutions should verify against consolidated EU sanctions list for full implementation context
- Original filename suggested Russia sanctions but actual document concerns Hamas/Palestinian Islamic Jihad sanctions under Regulation (EU) 2024/386
