## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: EU member states, financial institutions, entities transacting with designated persons, EU operators doing business with listed Chinese and Iranian cyber actors
- **shift_type**: expanded
- **shift_summary**: The EU expands its cyber-sanctions list by adding two Chinese individuals and three entities (including an Iranian cyber actor) responsible for significant cyber-attacks against Union critical infrastructure.

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Cybersecurity (state-sponsored cyber-attacks, APT activity)
- Critical infrastructure resilience (targets of listed actors include critical infrastructure sectors)

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**:
  - Asset freeze and prohibition on making funds available to: Chen Cheng (COO, Anxun Information Technology), Wu Haibo (CEO, Anxun Information Technology), Anxun Information Technology Co. Ltd., Integrity Technology Group, Emennet Pasargad
  - EU persons and entities must not transact with or provide resources to listed parties
  - Member states must enforce asset-freeze measures immediately
- **evidence_requirements**:
  - Designation backed by evidence of involvement in APT Flax Typhoon attacks (Integrity Technology Group) and "hacking-for-hire" operations targeting EU critical infrastructure (Anxun)
  - Iranian actor Emennet Pasargad designated for targeting EU democratic processes and digital infrastructure
- **enforcement_signal**: mandatory
