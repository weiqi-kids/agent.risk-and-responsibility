## L1 — Rule Signal
- **rule_type**: final
- **issuing_body**: NIST
- **document_id**: CSWP 42, NISTIR 8350, SP 1800-36
- **status**: final

## L2 — Responsibility Structure
- **affected_roles**: IoT device manufacturers, network administrators, IoT deployment teams, credential management teams, device lifecycle managers, industry partners
- **shift_type**: new
- **shift_summary**: 發布三份 IoT 安全接入最終出版物,要求在 IoT 設備連接網路前建立信任機制並提供唯一網路憑證,透過可擴展自動化的信任網路層接入方式管理設備完整生命週期

## L3 — Risk Domains
- Unauthorized IoT network connections
- Credential provisioning security
- IoT device trust establishment
- Network-device trust relationships
- IoT device lifecycle management
- Scalable automated credential management

## L4 — Obligation & Evidence
- **new_obligations**: Establish trust between networks and IoT devices before credential provisioning; use trusted network-layer onboarding mechanisms; provide IoT devices with unique network credentials through scalable, automated, trusted methods; manage devices throughout their complete lifecycles; protect against unauthorized network connections
- **evidence_requirements**: Trust establishment protocols; credential provisioning logs; network-layer onboarding implementation; unique credential assignment records; device lifecycle management documentation; unauthorized connection prevention measures
- **enforcement_signal**: recommended

## Notes
Three final publications released after four-year engagement with industry partners involving several builds and draft releases. Publications provide comprehensive approach to IoT secure onboarding applicable broadly across IoT device types. Document IDs: NIST Cybersecurity White Paper (CSWP) 42, NIST Internal Report 8350, and NIST Special Publication (SP) 1800-36.
