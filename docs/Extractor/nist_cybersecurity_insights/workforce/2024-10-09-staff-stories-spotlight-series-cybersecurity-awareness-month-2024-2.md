# Staff Stories Spotlight Series: Cybersecurity Awareness Month 2024 - Jeff Marron

## L1 — Core Facts

This blog is part of NIST's Cybersecurity Awareness Month 2024 Staff Stories Spotlight series, featuring Jeff Marron, an IT Specialist at NIST. The 2024 theme is "Secure our World."

Jeff Marron began his professional career as an elementary English as a Second Language teacher before transitioning into cybersecurity nearly 20 years ago through Department of Defense security training work. He worked at the FDA for approximately a decade before joining NIST in 2016.

As a cybersecurity researcher at NIST, Marron focuses on healthcare cybersecurity, Internet of Things (IoT) security, energy sector guidance, and small business cybersecurity support. Recent work includes:
- Updated HIPAA Security Rule guidance
- IoT product development cybersecurity handbook
- Secure IoT device provisioning guidance
- Smart inverter cybersecurity recommendations

## L2 — Context & Background

Marron's entry into cybersecurity came through a friend's consulting company connection, leading to Department of Defense security training work. His career trajectory from education to federal cybersecurity illustrates non-traditional pathways into the field.

The blog presents Q&A-style content about staff backgrounds, current projects, and perspectives on the "Secure our World" theme. Marron's work spans multiple critical infrastructure sectors and addresses both enterprise and consumer-facing security challenges.

## L3 — Implications & Analysis

Marron's career path demonstrates that cybersecurity expertise can be developed through practical experience rather than exclusively through formal technical education. His transition from education to security work highlights transferable skills in communication and training.

His research portfolio reflects NIST's cross-sector approach to cybersecurity guidance:
- **Healthcare:** HIPAA Security Rule updates address evolving threats to protected health information
- **IoT:** Consumer router and device security guidance targets the expanding attack surface of connected devices
- **Energy:** Smart inverter recommendations address critical infrastructure vulnerabilities
- **Small Business:** Recognition that security guidance must be accessible and practical for resource-constrained organizations

The breadth of his work areas aligns with the "Secure our World" theme, addressing interconnected security challenges across healthcare, energy, consumer technology, and small businesses—all essential infrastructure in modern society.
